Sarah Howard

Botanical illustrator

based in Norfolk, UK.

Biography

It was in Ethiopia, where I was living at the time, that I discovered how much fun it was to study plants as one drew and painted them. To provide a visual description, a record of great beauty, and to uncover hidden aspects of plants, underlies my approach to botanical painting. The Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh provided me over the years with training, topped with a Diploma (Distinction) in 2014. From 1997 to 2000 I was Illustrator for the Flora of Ethiopia & Eritrea (completed in 2009) producing black & white illustrations from herbarium material. On the side, I made colour paintings of endemic flowers, which is an ongoing project to help inform local and foreign residents of Ethiopia’s unique flora. In the UK I have contributed to joint exhibitions, provided classes in flower painting and worked on several commissions for private and public collections. I now move between Norfolk, UK and Ethiopia.
